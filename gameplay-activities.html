Gameplay Activities

Learning Through Play

Critical Play Sessions

Throughout the term we will be engaging in critical play sessions during class time to explore, experiment with, analyze, and critique a range of tabletop games. The embodied and affective dimensions of play can elicit powerful feelings — of joy, animosity, or even vulnerability — which students may not usually encounter in the classroom.

Our gameplay activities are designed to furnish a critical perspective on tabletop games as media and culture, and we will strive to create an environment for exploring alternate play practices that embrace difference — these activities are never about winning.

As such, in-class gameplay activities should be undertaken in a critically engaged spirit that welcomes participation from players of all backgrounds and from all levels of gaming experience or ability.

Design Project Playtesting

This semester we will engage in several rounds of focused playtesting with our tabletop game designs. These playtesting sessions are a crucial element of the design process and important opportunities to gather feedback from our peers that will inform the iterative process of our designs.

When presenting our creative work to our peers, it is also important to maintain a rigorous and productive attitude when offering feedback. It is equally important that this feedback be offered in a spirit of intellectual generosity, and playful curiosity. Doing so will ensure that these sessions help us build a supportive and dynamic community in our classroom.

This means that these sessions are very high-priority dates for attendance. For our design ideas to shine, they need to benefit from the engaged scrutiny that comes from repeated playtesting.

341 CON

R 12/10 · 4:00–7:00 PM · Greenlaw Gameroom (GL 316)

The course culminates in 341 CON, a public exhibition where students debut the original tabletop games they have designed, prototyped, and playtested over the second half of the semester. Visitors come to the Gameroom to play the games, talk with the designers, and offer feedback.

Members of the UNC community and the public are warmly invited. Come play, offer feedback, and build community around the table.
