Overview and Outline

Course Description and Weekly Schedule of Readings + Activities

Description

This section of ENGL‑341 treats tabletop games as what they are — cultural objects with histories, meanings, and consequences. We’ll analyze the mechanics and thematics that tabletop games use to create narratives, paying particular attention to the values and stories that emerge from the decisions designers make.

To do this, we will approach tabletop games in three ways. First, by exploring the material histories of tabletop games; whether as ancient artifacts, toys for children, simulations for soldiers, or cultural commodities of the modern hobby industry. Second, by interrogating the unique narrative affordances of tabletop games; considering how designers compose materials, images, rules, and players around the table within a text of gameplay. Finally, by practicing effective strategies for designing tabletop games; crafting original game designs that generate a range of different didactic, aesthetic, and social encounters. Along the way, we’ll also attend closely to the ways tabletop games influence players’ feelings about each other and the shared worlds that they inhabit — playing (and re-designing) some of the most culturally loaded games ever made.

The course culminates in a capstone project in which students debut their own original tabletop games at “341 CON,” a public exhibition for the UNC community and beyond. No prior experience with tabletop gaming is required — but you’ll leave with some.
